Extracted Annotations (4/22/2020, 3:07:26 PM)

“Random Multi-group SIGR Model” (Demongeot et al 2012:1159)

“Susceptible contains persons likely to have disease” (Demongeot et al 2012:1159)

“second compartment contains Infected” (Demongeot et al 2012:1159)

“last compartment is revealed to the recovered people” (Demongeot et al 2012:1159)

“third compartment, there is people Infectious with, e.g., gametocytæmia that is to say, people who are contagious” (Demongeot et al 2012:1159)

“Table 1 Definition of the parameters used in the system bkj: transmission coefficients between compartments Sk and Gj dkS : natural-death rate in the k-th group of the S compartment dk : natural-death rate in the k-th group of the I compartment dkG : natural-death rate in the k-th group of the G compartment dkR : natural-death rate in the k-th group of the R compartment ek: disease-caused death rate in the k-th group of the I compartment xk: disease-caused death rate in the k-th group of the R compartment Kk: influx of new individuals in the k-th group pk: fraction of new individuals in the k-th group hk: fraction of vaccine individuals in the k-th group gk: rate at which an infected becomes infectious in the k-th group ck: recovery rate of individuals in the k-th group” (Demongeot et al 2012:1160)

Ref

J. Demongeot, O. Hansen, A. S. Jannot, and C. Taramasco, “Random Modelling of Contagious (Social and Infectious) Diseases: Examples of Obesity and HIV and Perspectives Using Social Networks,” in 2012 26th International Conference on Advanced Information Networking and Applications Workshops, Fukuoka, Japan, Mar. 2012, pp. 1153–1160, doi: 10.1109/WAINA.2012.173.
